# Product

## Register

product

## Platform

web

## Users

Developers, designers, and tech-savvy visitors evaluating the frontend engineering and visual craft of a portfolio showcase. The user wants to play a quick game of Snake that feels tactile, highly responsive, and looks exceptionally polished.

## Product Purpose

A showcase Snake game that demonstrates meticulous attention to detail, clean visual design, and premium user experience, proving that even a simple classic game can look and feel like a modern, publication-grade piece of design.

## Positioning

An interactive showcase of minimalist, warm-editorial design applied to retro arcade gameplay, proving frontend craft is in the execution, typography, and detail.

## Brand Personality

Sophisticated, warm-editorial, minimalist, clean, with flat-color pastel accents. Tactile, premium, and calm rather than overstimulating.

## Anti-references

- Sloppy, sketchy SVG doodles or amateurish vector assets.
- SaaS cliché layouts, metric boxes, or template-like dashboard cards.
- Default 2026 cream/sand-colored slop backgrounds that look like generic AI outputs.
- Over-stimulating, flashing neon-dark cyberpunk screens.

## Design Principles

- **Print-inspired Typography**: Use Outfit and JetBrains Mono with clean hierarchy, precise letter spacing, and deliberate line heights to evoke a premium editorial layout.
- **Curated Pastel Strategy**: Employ solid, curated pastel hues (such as terracotta, sage green, and muted rose) with high legibility, rather than generic primary colors or neon glows.
- **Tactile Material Feel**: Buttons, cells, and states should react with immediate, physical-feeling transitions, giving the player instant feedback on keyboard and click inputs.
- **Negative Space as Luxury**: Avoid cluttering the screen with unnecessary boxes or borders. Let the layout breathe with ample whitespace and clear spatial hierarchy.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- Clear keyboard focus indicators and support for standard keys (Arrow keys + WASD).
- Contrast ratios exceeding WCAG AA standards (>= 4.5:1 for body and grid indicators).
- Respect standard user preferences including `prefers-reduced-motion` for all transitions.
